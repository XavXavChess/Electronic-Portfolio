My Clubs and Activities

Chess Club

Treasurer

As the Treasurer of the Chess Club, I manage our club funds and budget for tournaments and equipment. I coordinate with other club officers to ensure we have the resources needed for our activities. Chess has been a passion of mine since elementary school, and I enjoy both the strategic thinking and the community we've built in our club.

I help organize weekly club meetings where members can practice, learn new strategies, and prepare for competitions. We also host intramural tournaments throughout the school year.

Science Olympiad

Member (2023-4)

As a member of the Science Olympiad team, I participate in various STEM-based competitions that test knowledge, problem-solving skills, and creativity. I specialize in physics and engineering events, collaborating with teammates to design and build devices for competition.

Science Olympiad has significantly enhanced my technical skills and scientific knowledge while allowing me to work alongside peers who share my passion for science and innovation. Our team regularly practices and prepares for regional and state competitions.

Key Club

Club Representative (2023-4)

As a proud member and Club Representative of Key Club, I help coordinate community service projects and represent our club at district meetings. I've participated in numerous service events including the Kiwanis Family Dance and Kroctoberfest at the Croc Center.

Through Key Club, I've developed leadership skills while making a positive impact in our community. I help recruit new members and communicate club activities to the student body. Our service projects focus on addressing local needs and supporting international initiatives.

National Technical Honor Society

Member

As a member of the National Technical Honor Society, I uphold the seven attributes of our organization: Skill, Honesty, Service, Responsibility, Scholarship, Citizenship, and Leadership. Membership recognizes my academic achievements and technical skills.

NTHS has connected me with like-minded peers who excel in career and technical education. We participate in community service projects and professional development opportunities that strengthen our technical skills and prepare us for future careers or higher education in technical fields.

AOIT/Cyber Council

Junior Representative

As Junior Representative on the AOIT/Cyber Council, I represent the interests and perspectives of junior students in our Academy of Information Technology. I participate in council meetings where we discuss program improvements, upcoming events, and industry partnerships.

This leadership role has given me valuable experience in communication and advocacy. I collaborate with faculty advisors and industry professionals to enhance our program's curriculum and ensure it remains cutting-edge in the rapidly evolving technology field.
